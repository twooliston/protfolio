The 2 main projects which I found worth mentioning are 2 final year projects. My dissertation and my Entrepreneurship group project.

1. Dissertation Project

My dissertation project took a look the strain food waste has on the planet and how it could be tackled and reduced. Being linked to air, water and soil pollution, I decided that food waste would be a positive topic to work on during my final year, in light of the growing discussion and increased awareness of environmental issues.

The Work

The aim of the project was to:
  1. Increase user awareness on the impact food waste has on the planet
  2. Persuade the user to reduce the amount of food they waste
  3. Develop a system that facilitates the user reducing their food waste
Therefore, my paper researches the use of persuasive technology and behaviour change to reach these aims. The system was developed to support a community style marketplace to exchange goods for free while integrating persuasive features to encourage the users to reduce their food waste. Through prototyping with free software I came across online, I was able to play with the design of my product to meet my requirements.

What I Gained

Starting and finishing a project on my own has been a great source of experience and am now comfortable with the new languages, frameworks and software that I decided to use. In addition, being more interested in the design aspect of product development, I enjoyed being fully in control and dedicated a large portion of my project into the HCI research and how to improve the user experience with guidance from engagement with stakeholders and potential users.

2. Entrepreneurship Group Project

This project had an aim of coming up with a new product to compete in the real world market and closer related to a business or management module. The project was split into three sections where we had to research the market, develop a prototype for the project and decide upon a strategy to become successful in the chosen market with the team's product.

The Product

After some brainstorming, we came ot the agreement that our best idea was to build an application similar to Tinder but with the goal of finding people (or groups of people) with similar hobbies and encourage them to organise and actively participate in activities in relation to those hobbies.

What I Gained

Just like any group project, the team had to be managed and deal with any issues that may arise, hence, a good team work practice. In addition, taking 3 management/business related modules for the first time has provided me with an insight into the field allowing me to better consider all the factors that may play a part the success of a business.
